date = "2026-06-23"
+++

I didn't grow up needing to read an analog clock quickly or often. They were
always present, and I knew how to read them, but I preferred digital clocks.

It takes me about 10 seconds to read an analog clock, whether at full precision
or just to get a sense of the time.

Sometimes however, if an analog clock is in view, even without me trying or
thinking about it, a thought will appear somewhere on the desk of my mind which
reads, "It's 9:42".

I'll think, "Why, and how, did I know that?" and then I'll see the analog clock,
take 10 seconds to confirm, and realize what's going on.

I have no idea why this happens.
